Research Data Management

Learning goal: Understand what personal is and what falls under the special categories of data.

‘Personal data’ referrs to any information related to an identified or identialble natural person.

This means personal data is any information on a living person. This information can be objective or subjective. This information can differentiate one individual from another and says something about them.

  • In general, if you are using data collected from/ about people, it is aleays best to assume it is personal data or else get expert advise

In the GDPR, there is another level of data we must be aware of.

This is the special categories of personal data. When working with special categories of personal data there are stricter conditions. Contact your Privacy Champion if you think this applies to your research.

  • Racial or ethnic origin
  • Political opinions
  • Religion or philosophical beliefs
  • Trade union membership
  • Health
    • Including any type of physical measurement, assessment of mental well-being or cognitive function, even in a non-clinical population
  • Sex life or sexual orientation
  • Personal data concering criminal convictions and personal data concerning unlawful or objectionable conduct for which a ban has been imposed
  • Biomentric data (fingerprints, iris-scans)
  • Genetic data
